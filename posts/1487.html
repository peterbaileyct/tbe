Update: Charter revision recommendations 2019 and the arts magnet school

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=1487 Published on 2019-08-08 15:18:47

By David Fortier

Jack Krampitz and David Fortier discuss two topics during this 24 minute podcast.

The first has to do with the recommendations of the Charter Revision Commission's recommendations for this year's ballot. 2019 is a municipal election year. We vote Tuesday, November 5.

The second has to do with Memorial Boulevard Intradistrict Magnet Arts School.

Admittedly, the conversation is not the most riveting, but it is important to have an idea what is happening with these two initiatives.

Of course, the charter commission's work is approved by the city council. The magnet arts school is under the aegis of the board of education.

Typically, charter revisions are considered every two years. In the discussion, Fortier refers to Krampitz's involvement the last time around--this is Krampitz's second time around on the commission.

More information about the commission's work can be found on the City of Bristol website.

The intradistrict arts magnet school is moving ahead with the goal of meetings a 2022 deadline. Board of education committees, which meet regularly, are close to approving building plans and programs for students. More information is available on the website dedicated to the project.
