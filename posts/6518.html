Bristol Today -- The day in review

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=6518 Published on 2021-07-19 18:46:01

Public Library accepting book returns indoors

Bristol Public Library is now accepting books returns inside. Colorful notices taped to the outdoor return boxes announced the change in policy, beginning today. During the coronavirus pandemic, all books were returned at the outdoor boxes to ensure the safety of staff and the public. (Photo by D. Fortier)

Little League fields under water after weekend storms

Heavy rains this weekend saturated the water table the Little League fields on Mix St., resulting in flooding. (Photos by R. Carter)

Memorial Boulevard bridge construction backs up traffic, weekend storms may have damaged street lights

Motorists heading west back in to Bristol faced heavy traffic Monday at 4:30PM. With the bridge out on both sides of the Boulevard, drivers have to basically choose driving back into town on South Street or on Riverside Avenue. (Photo by J. Krampitz)
Multiple intersections were impacted by Saturday-Sunday night electrical storms, according to a Department of Transportation worker. While the signals are working, they are in "recall" and their suggested timings are not being used.
In particular, the Middle St.-Mountain Rd.-Pine St. intersection has a pedestrian signal being called every cycle which is a 25-second loss to traffic from Mountain Rd.
It is DOT's understanding that the system components were damaged by the storm and repairs are to have been completed today. (Photo by J Krampitz)

Noon-time accident at Frederick and Andrews streets

A noon-time accident forced the closing of lanes in both directions at the intersection of Frederick and Andrews streets. (Photo by R. Carter)
