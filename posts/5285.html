Bristol Central girls track drops 81-68 decision to Lewis Mills

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=5285 Published on 2021-04-30 15:30:57

By Michael Letendre

BURLINGTON - The Bristol Central girls track team was in a close duel against Lewis Mills on Tuesday, April 27 in Burlington.

The locals had several first place finishers but, in the end, the Rams fell by an 81-68 final in a CCC South confrontation to fall to 1-2 overall on the year.

Central had multiple winners on the track, including Natalia Popielarz and Salvatrice Bosco.

The 4x800 relay team of Isabella Crandall, Sophia Stark, Popielarz and Bosco won the grueling event in 10:52.

And then Popielarz ruled the afternoon in both the 800 meter and mile challenges.

Popielarz posted a 2:37.8 in the half-mile event while in the 1600 meters, the senior standout came in first place with a time of 6:04.  

Bosco zipped up a leading time of 60.9 seconds in the 400 while in the 200 meter sprint, the senior won the event in 27.9 seconds. 

Finally in the shot put, Kaleah Richards made a winning toss of 22 feet, 2 and ¾ inches.

Mills improved its season ledger to 2-1 off the winning effort.

On Tuesday, May 4, Central is home in a CCC South encounter against Middletown from the BC track.
