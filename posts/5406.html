NAACP Press Conference calls for U.S. Justice Department, State and local police to investigate hate crimes

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=5406 Published on 2024-11-20 09:23:08

Scot X Esdaile, State President of the Connecticut NAACP, and Corrie Betts, Chairman Of the Connecticut NAACP, announced at a Press Conference on Friday, May 7, that The CT State Conference of the NAACP has called on the U.S. Justice Department, the Windsor Mayor, Town Council, and State officials to address the recent discovery of several Nooses in an Amazon site in Windsor.

Mr. Esdaile stressed that these forms of Hate Crimes must be investigated, and the perpetrators must be held accountable to the fullest extent of the law. He added that racial acts of terrorism are an utter reminder of the current state of bigotry in America. “White supremacists often attempt to diminish and hinder black people's opportunity in this nation. This can not be tolerated,” said Mr Esdaile.

Founded on February 12, 1909, the NAACP is the nation’s foremost, largest, and most widely recognized civil rights organization. Its more than half-million members and supporters throughout the United States and the world are the premier advocates for civil rights in their communities, leading grassroots campaigns for equal opportunity and conducting voter mobilization.
