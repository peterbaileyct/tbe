Rockwell Concert Series continued Tuesday with big crowd to hear Latanya Farrell

By Jack Krampitz

Latanya Farrell was the featured performer at the Rockwell Concert Series Tuesday night, and she certainly did not disappoint.
Farrell, who also serves as the Principal of Stafford School, delivered a nice mix of jazz, soul, and pop favorites, and had the large audience singing and dancing along with her throughout the night.
One of the highlights of the evening was her rendition of the song “Fly Like an Eagle.” While Farrell was singing, a large contingent of her Stafford students and teachers danced along with school’s mascot, the eagle.
I cannot resist an editorial comment. When I was in the third grade, I can’t remember going to a concert where my principal sang to a huge audience. Then again, my principal was a member of the Sisters of Saint Joseph, and she had a hard time singing “Tantum Ergo,” never mind “Fly Like an Eagle.” She also lacked the charisma Farrell exhibited throughout a delightful evening.

COVID Vaccination locations in Bristol

At last night’s City Council meeting, Bristol residents who have not been vaccinated were encouraged to go to one many locations where the vaccine is available. All of the following places require no appointment.
  • Bristol Health Clinic, 41 Brewster Rd.
  • Walgreens Pharmacy, 525 Farmington Ave.
  • Bristol-Burlington Health District Clinic, 240 Stafford Ave.
  • Walgreens Pharmacy, 25 Main St.
  • Price Chopper Pharmacy, 121 Farmington Ave.
  • CVS Pharmacy, 839 Farmington Ave.
  • Stop & Shop, 747 Pine St.
  • Walmart Pharmacy, 1400 Farmington Ave.
  • CVS Pharmacy, 81 North St.
Also, there are special event clinics:
  • Lake Compounce (every Saturday)
  • Bristol Central and Bristol Eastern (1 week before the start of school)
