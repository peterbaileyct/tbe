Bristol Today- the day in review July 24

Originally found at https://www.bristoledition.org/?p=6708 Published on 2021-07-24 17:51:23

Farmer’s Market has largest crowd of the year

The farmers market had its largest crowds of the year this morning, as the beautiful weather drew many Bristolites to Centre Square. Several of the vendors said today’s crowd was the biggest by far.
The other factor beside the great day is that corn, tomatoes, and other produce are all in-season now, so the farms have a great selection of all fruits and vegetables. And the smell of fresh apple-cider donuts don’t hurt, either.

Bristol Blues home at Muzzy tonight

If you are looking for something to do tonight or tomorrow, the Blues have a game at 6:30 tonight with Mystic and face off against Newport Sunday at 5:30. The Blues have turned around their season with great play recently, so it’s a good time to head to Muzzy Field to see them compete.

Bristol Little League Seniors advance to state championship round

With a 19-9 win over Thomaston Friday night, the Bristol Little League Seniors team advanced to the state championship round.
The Bristol seniors program is vying for its fifth state title, capturing championships in2013, 2014, 2015, 2017, and 2018.
Bristol plays the winner of the Stonington/Pawcatuck Vs. Plainfield, which plays tonight. 
The championship starts  Tuesday in Pawcatuck at 5:30.
